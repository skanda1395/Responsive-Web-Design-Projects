Michael Jordan

Michael takes "The Last Shot" in Game 6 of the 1998 NBA Finals.

Before he took flight

  • Michael played all sorts of games growing up before he focused on basketball.
  • He was 5'11" when he tried out for the varsity baskeball team during his sophomore year, but he didn't make the team.
  • He proved himself over the summer when he averaged 25 ppg when he played for the varsity team.

In College

  • Under Dean Smith's coaching, he was named ACC freshman of the year when he averaged 13.4 ppg (points per game) on 53.4% shooting (field goal percentage).
  • He made the game-winning jump shot, which he's notable for, in the 1982 NCAA Championship game against Georgetown, which was led by future NBA rival Patrick Ewing.

In the NBA

  • From the moment he set foot on an NBA court Michael Jordan dominated his sport like few athletes ever had. He had skill and style. He turned the Chicago Bulls into a dynasty.
  • He had great initial years, averaging 37.1 points on 48.2% shooting, but didn't make the playoffs until the 90's.
  • He had problems getting past Pistons for three years until 1990-91 season when they swept them and entered their first NBA Finals.
  • His first finals was against the Magic Johnson led Los Angeles Lakers and he won his first NBA championship and never looked back.
  • The Bulls dominated the NBA, winning three-peat, until '93 when Jordan's father died in an accident and he retired in 1993.
  • He came back with the "I'm back" retort for the 1993-1994 season. And, the Bulls would contiue to dominate and make their second three-peat.
